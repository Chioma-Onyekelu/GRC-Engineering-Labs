# **Task 1: Establishing the Security Policy Hierarchy**

**Q1. Hierarchy Definition Table**

| Document Type | Purpose | Characteristics | Approval Authority |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Policy | Defines the organization's high-level security objectives and direction. | Broad, mandatory, focuses on what must be done, strategic in nature. | Executive leadership or Board (e.g., CEO, Steering Committee). |
| Standard | Specifies mandatory requirements that support policies. | More detailed than policies, mandatory, measurable and enforceable. | Information Security Manager or IT Governance Committee. |
| Guideline | Provides recommended best practices to achieve security objectives. | Advisory, flexible, not mandatory. | Security or IT leadership. |
| Procedure | Provides step-by-step instructions for performing tasks. | Detailed, sequential, operational, focuses on how tasks are performed. | IT management or operational supervisors. |

3.  **Prohibited Activities**   
   Users must not carry out illegal activities or usage of the company systems, distributing malicious programs nor accessing inappropriate or offensive content.   
     
4. **Protection of Company Data**   
   Users must safeguard all sensitive company and client data. Confidential information may only be disclosed with explicit authorization.  
     
5. **Personal Use**  
   Personal use of company devices is also permitted to a limited extent provided that it does not hinder work duties or risk safekeeping.

* Employees and Users. When using company resources, must also adhere to the Acceptable Use Policy.  
* IT and Security Teams. Monitoring compliance and applying security controls.  
* Human Resources. Coordinating disciplinary processes or other corrective actions in the event of violation of AUP.

**Task 3: User Access Request Procedure**   
**Q1. NexusTech Solutions User Access Request Procedure** 

1. **Purpose**   
   The purpose of this procedure is to ensure that all user access to NexusTech systems is requested, approved, and provisioned in a secure and controlled manner.   
2.  **Scope**   
   This procedure applies to all new user accounts and access requests for NexusTech information systems.   
3. **Prerequisites**   
   Before initiating this procedure, the following must be available:   
* Access to the IT Service Management (ITSM) ticketing system  
* Authorization from the requesting manager   
* Administrative access to Active Directory or relevant system platforms  
4. **Procedure Steps**   
1. **Step 1: Receiving the Request** 

   A formal IT ticket is newly required with the user access request, including: *name of employee needing access,* *role in company; department;* and *systems or environments* needed for the employee to do their job**.** 

2. **Step 2: Verifying Approval** 

   It is the Helpdesk technician’s responsibility to verify that the request has been approved by both the employee’s department manager as well as H.R. 

3. **Step 3: Creating the Account** 

   The Helpdesk technician will create a user account in Active Directory or relevant system using standardized naming convention. 

4. **Step 4: Assigning Permissions** 

   Access permissions are limited and assigned to employees solely on the basis of their job role according to least privilege enforcement. 

5. **Step 5: Notifying the User** 

   When the account is created, the Helpdesk technician will email login information and instructions to the employee and their manager.

6.  **Step 6: Documenting the Action** 

   The actions are all logged by the Helpdesk technician in the ticketing system, and once verified, the request is closed. 

5. **Exceptions** 

Emergency access requests may be approved by senior IT management. These requests must be documented and reviewed within 24 hours.
